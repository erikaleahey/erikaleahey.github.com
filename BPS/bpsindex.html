Boston Pick-Up Sports has both a website and an app to help users stay connected and organize games.

Users can go to bostonpickupsports.com to register. There, they can search for games to join, or they can organize their own. Users can create match specifications using categories such as: time, date, location, skill level, competitive level, and age. Setting up games is completely free, but there are also options to have paid games for more competitive players that wish to utilize BPS' referee services.

The real-time application allows users to check notifications, create and view events on the fly, and record the stats of their matches.

WHY

YOUNG

ADULTS?

Boston is unique, as it is home to over fifty colleges. As a college town, there is a much larger young adult population than in other cities. While Boston has a lot of great programs catered to the young adults, and plenty of intramural teams, there is no service that offers a low commitment, free option to meet other people and play pick-up games. Most of organizations catering to meeting new people are dating sites, and the services BPS provides are strictly platonic. The objective of BPS is to stress free and fun as possible.

Research also revealed that a shocking number of people that played sports in high school don't continue to do so afterwards. BPS aims to provide an alternative to the hyper-competitve atmosphere that college sports foster.

DEMOGRAPHIC DATA: BOSTON

101, 540 people are currently enrolled in college or graduate school in Boston.

One in three Boston residents is a young adult.

Boston gains around 10,000 new people each year.

In 2001, Boston's young adults had total expenditures exceeding $3.4 billion—with $1.3 billion spent on housing, $382 million on entertainment and food away from home, and $173 million on apparel and services.

Boston's share of the state's young adult population has increased from 12.7% to 14.6% in the last two decades.

DEMOGRAPHIC DATA: NATIONAL

One out of five Americans is classified as a young adult.

20–34 year olds constitute 20.3% of the population.

In 2010, 4,455,740 males and 3,125,637 females played athletics in high school.

In the same year, 249,307 males and 186,460 females played NCAA sports.
