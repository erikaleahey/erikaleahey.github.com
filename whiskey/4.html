Whiskey Behavior

Most modern distilleries use a continuous still to distill their wash. A continuous still consists of a tall cylindrical column filled with perforated plates. Steam enters from the bottom, and the distiller's beer enters from the top.

The beer is distilled as it slowly drips through the plates, and the alcohol is condensed back into liquid. The product is called "low wine", which is then distilled a second time to produce "high wine", or whiskey that contains about 70% alcohol.
